Mumbai: MMRDA fails to recover premium dues to the tune of Rs 866 cr, says CAG:-

TNN | Mar 28, 2018, 21:10 IST

Mumbai: The Mumbai Metropolitan Region Development Authority’s (MMRDA) failure to recover lease premium on schedule resulted in non-recovery of Rs 866 crore, states the Comptroller and Auditor General’s report 2017 tabled in both houses on the last day of the state legislature. In 2008 the state urban development department announced an increase in floor space index for residential (from 1 to 3) and commercial (2 to 4) exploitation in the G Block in the Bandra-Kurla Complex. This resulted in an additional Built Up Area of 13 lakh sq metres becoming available in G Block. This was made available to existing allottees at 100 and 150% respectively of the Ready Reckoner Rate as a one-time scheme. As per the scheme, 20% of the premium was to be paid along with demand letter before March 2009 (revised to March 2010 and further revised to March 2011) and rest in four annual instalments with 10 per cent simple interest. The delay in payment of annual instalment would attract delayed interest at 14%. There was no time limit specified for consumption of the additional BUA.

As per the scheme, 20% of the premium was to be paid along with demand letter before March 2009 (revised to March 2010 and further revised to March 2011) and rest in four annual instalments with 10 per cent simple interest. The delay in payment of annual instalment would attract delayed interest at 14%. There was no time limit specified for consumption of the additional BUA. The CAG studied two cases and found that M/s Reliance Industry that was allotted an additional BUA of 67,000 sq metres for Rs 985 crore did not adhere to the time schedule for instalment payment. By March 2016, the sum had touched Rs 1,340 crore but the allottee had paid only Rs 697 crore.

CBSE Re-examination:

All you need to know about who needs to write the exam, what next and possible roll back New Delhi: Central Board of Secondary Education, CBSE taking cognizance of reports of the paper leak today afternoon issued a notice announcing the re-examination of Mathematics paper for class 10th and economics examination of class 12th board examinations 2018. Immediately after the rollout of the news reactions of parents, students, teachers and celebrities started to flood the internet. As the WhatsApp groups activated with the news more information started to flood. But what next? Who all need to write the exam? Does it apply to students outside Delhi as well? Is there a rollback? These are the questions that are worrying people the most and we have tried to answer them here.

What next? The other big question for students and parents is what next. The answer, though painful, is fairly simple. The students and parents are required to keep a lookout for the schedule of the re-examination. The dates of the re-examination would be announced by the CBSE before the end of the week. Experts are suggesting that though there is no precedence, in all probability CBSE may conduct the Class 10 mathematics paper by April 6 and Class 12 Economics paper by April 14 or 17. As to the exact date, there is no confirmation yet. “In the normal course of events, CBSE Class 10 board examinations 2018 were scheduled to end on April 4 with Painting and the last exam for CBSE Class 12 was of Physical Education on April 13. CBSE in all probability would conduct the re-examination only after the end of the normal scheduled examinations. As to paper setting and printing, that should not be a problem.” Another expert agreed. However, the board would be deciding the dates and releasing them shortly. In the meanwhile, all the teachers have urged the students not to get disheartened or lose hope and keep the preperations on.

Who all need to appear in the CBSE class 10 and 12 Re-examination? Starting off with the basics – to all who are concerned, CBSE conducts only one examination for All India and Delhi Region. Till last year the papers used to be different. However, from this year, the question papers for pan India are same. In effect, the decision to conduct a re-examination applies to all the candidates who have written the examination. For all the regions – Delhi as well as every other state. HRD Minister has confirmed in the press conference that the re-examination is for all. Though it has been confirmed by Union HRD Minister Prakash Javadekar that the instance of the leak was reported from a few regions in Delhi, the impact of the retest is pan India. All the students who have enrolled for CBSE Board Examinations 2018 and have written the Mathematics and Economics paper for Class 10 and 12 are required to appear in the re-examination. For all practical purposes, students can understand that the paper conducted today for Class 10 mathematics and the paper conducted on March 26 for class 12 economics now stands cancelled. Times AI Hub's Conclave Captures Predictive Policing Techniques

Industry experts discuss the ways and means Artificial Intelligence is being used in different industries successfully at Times AI Hub recent event. March 27, 2018, NOIDA : After successfully hosting Artificial Intelligence-focussed events at the prestigious IIT Delhi and IIIT Okhla, Times AI Hub recently organised it's third Artificial Intelligence Conclave at IISc Bengaluru. Times AI Hub is an Artificial Intelligence (AI) focussed virtual platform that connects the industry thinkers, practitioners, companies and influencers, and keeps hosting events to formalise dialogues in the AI domain With this conclave, the recently formed AI Hub's Bengaluru chapter intended to bring together all industry leaders on a common platform to share knowledge and interact. The conclave witnessed exhaustive discussions on practical implications of Artificial Intelligence (AI) & Machine Learning for enterprises and organisations, and the solutions which enhance business productivity.

With a gathering of about 250+ participants from companies like, Rolls Royce, Mercedes Benz, Bangalore International Airport, IBM, NPCI, Deloitte Consulting, Genpact, Columbia Asia, and HCL Technologies, this event was a huge success. Some of the eminent speakers included Sundara Nagalingam, Head of Deep Learning at NVIDIA India who shared his learnings on the topic AI in the physical world: Practical applications of Machine Learning. Other speakers included Dr. Pratibha Moogi, Part Head, Media Analytics & Recognition at Samsung Electronics, Dr. Manish Gupta, Director Machine Learning & Data Science - American Express, Dhaval Doshi, Founder - Smarthome NX, Sandeep Chanda, AVP Technology at Neudesic, Dr. Praphul Chandra, Founder Koinearth, Amit Singh, Director IoT at Cognizant Technology Solutions and Avi Patchava, VP, Data Strategy at Inmobi. The event's keynote was delivered by Ramit Sharma, Inspector General Police of Allahabad Range, who presented fascinating insights on Predictive Analysis, Crowd Management and Service Delivery using AI: Current Trends and Future Prospects. Incidentally, he is instrumental in developing an AI-based software known as agent-based modelling. With this, the Allahabad Range Police manage crowd behaviour more strategically, and this came in handy during the recent Kumbh Mela. The enthralling talk sessions upraised the audience about how AI and Machine Learning technologies are fast turning into tools for predictive policing. The panel discussion led by the moderator Dhaval Doshi was centered around the topic Artificial Intelligence 2018 & Beyond. It decoded the key reasons on how Artificial Intelligence, Internet of Things & Machine Learning are becoming integral to every industry and business. The panellists also presented case studies based on their experiences in their respective industry verticals, which was highly applauded by the audience. Expressing his happiness at the event turnout, Ramathreya Krishnamurthi, Business Head, TimesJobs & TechGig, said that new technologies will be an indispensable part of future work sphere and its right time to adopt this learning curve. "The future of work is tech-driven. At TimesJobs, we have been cautiously leading this dialogues through the Times AI Hub mantle. It's heartening to see so many leading companies, thinkers and leaders discuss this avenue passionately. The case studies presented here were phenomenal and these were my favourite part of the discussion," he said. Class X Maths and Class XII Economics exams

Crime Branch officials said that on March 23, the CBSE received by fax from an unknown source a complaint stating that Vicky, who runs the coaching institute in Rajendra Nagar, is involved in the leak of the question papers. The complaint also said two schools in the area were involved in the leak. The complaint was forwarded to the CBSE regional office in New Delhi the next day, and the office sent a copy of the complaint to a police inspector via WhatsApp the same day.
